# Glossary — the terms I use in the vault (so nothing reads like gibberish)

Figured I'd drop a quick glossary since some of the posts throw around words that aren't exactly everyday. If you hit a term in the vault you don't recognize, it's probably here.

## Bone & structure

- **Maxilla / maxillary** — the upper jaw bone. Huge deal in this whole thing because its position (forward vs back, up vs down) shapes the midface, the nose base, and how "projected" your face looks.
- **Mandible** — the lower jaw bone.
- **Nasion** — the point on your skull where the bridge of the nose meets the forehead (top of the nasal bones). Reference point for a lot of measurements.
- **Gonial angle** — the angle at the back corner of your lower jaw (where the jawbone turns up to the ear). Sharper/lower angle = more "square" jaw look; more open angle = softer, rounder jaw corner.
- **Cranial base** — the floor of the skull your face hangs off of. Its shape/angle (flexed vs flat) influences whether your face grows long or short, and pushes the upper face forward or back relative to the lower face.
- **Forward growth** — when the maxilla develops forward and down (good). The opposite is vertical/downward growth, which lengthens the face and recesses the midface.
- **Alar flare** — when the nostrils/outer nose wings spread wider, usually from the maxilla projecting forward and stretching the skin.

## Ratios & measurements

- **FWHR (Facial Width-to-Height Ratio)** — face width (cheekbone to cheekbone) divided by face height (hairline to chin). Higher = wider, more masculine-looking face.
- **Midface ratio** — how the middle third of the face (eyes to base of nose) relates to the rest. A shorter, tighter midface reads as more attractive.
- **ESR (Eye-Spacing Ratio / inter-canthal)** — relates the distance between your eyes to eye width. Too wide = "buggy" look; too narrow = crowded.
- **Cervicomental angle** — the angle where your neck meets your chin/jaw. Sharp angle = defined jaw-neck junction; obtuse/blunt = "weak" or double-chin look.
- **Facial fifths** — the idea that the face divides into 5 equal vertical sections (forehead, each eye zone, nose, lower face). Used to judge left-right balance and spacing.
- **Palatal / occlusal plane angle** — the tilt of the roof of your mouth / your bite plane. Its upward slope toward the front affects nose-tip rotation (upturned vs down-turned nose).

## Descriptive terms

- **Leanness** — low facial fat / defined subcutaneous tissue. The vault ranks this #1 because a lean face shows bone structure sharply; puffiness hides it.
- **Angularity** — how sharp vs soft the transitions of your face are (jaw corners, cheekbones, brow). Angular = defined edges; round = soft tissue dominance.
- **Dolichocephalic** — long, narrow skull/face type. Tends toward longer midface and more convex nose profile.
- **Brachycephalic** — short, wide skull/face type. Tends toward shorter midface, upturned/flat-bridge nose.
- **Craniofacial growth pattern** — the overall direction your face developed (vertical long-face vs horizontal wide-face), set early by the skull base and cranial structure.

## General

- **Midface** — the middle third of the face (under the eyes to the base of the nose). A well-developed, forward midface is one of the biggest attractiveness drivers.
- **Good vs bad comparison** — most posts show a side-by-side: a face/picture that displays the trait well vs poorly, so you can see the difference instead of guessing from words.

If I used a word in a post that's not on this list and it confused you, call it out and I'll add it.
