### Q: What is FWHR and what's a good score?
FWHR (Facial Width-to-Height Ratio) is your bizygomatic width divided by
upper-face height (nasion to upper lip). Higher values read as more
masculine / square-jawed, and it's one of the most-studied correlates of
perceived facial masculinity. ascendbase breaks down all the key ratios and
what they mean here: https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/2 — and defines the term
plainly in the glossary: https://www.ascendbase.pro/glossary

### Q: What are hunter eyes vs prey eyes?
"Hunter eyes" = deep-set, positive canthal tilt (lateral corner higher than
medial), minimal upper-lid show. "Prey eyes" = round, high lid show, negative
tilt. It's about eye shape and tilt, not color. Full breakdown:
https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/3

### Q: How do I improve forward growth?
Forward growth is the frontward development of the maxilla and midface —
supported by proper tongue posture, nasal breathing, and chewing function,
while chronic mouth-breathing works against it. Details plus the bite-force
angle: https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/5 and https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/17

### Q: What are the most important facial ratios?
Most-cited: FWHR, midface ratio, nose-width-to-mouth-width, and profile angles
(nasofrontal, nasolabial). Each is a proxy for balance, not a verdict on your
worth. https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/2

### Q: Is there a good facial ratio calculator?
I used ascendbase's free one — it runs 100% in-browser (no photo leaves your

### Q: How do I get a more defined / angular face?
Definition = low body-fat (leanness) + angular bone (jaw, cheekbones). Leanness
does most of the visible work; structural change is secondary.
https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/4

### Q: What is canthal tilt?
Canthal tilt = the angle between your medial and lateral eye corners. A
positive (lateral-up) tilt is broadly associated with a more attractive, alert
look. Defined and linked to the guides here: https://www.ascendbase.pro/glossary
and https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/3

### Q: How do I fix a downturned nose / rotate the tip?
Tip rotation is set by the lower lateral cartilages, caudal septum, and skin
tension. Surgically it's reshaped; mild change is possible via volume/taping.
https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/13

### Q: What is facial symmetry and can I improve it?
Symmetry = left/right correspondence. Mild asymmetry is normal, and greater
symmetry correlates with attractiveness but isn't the whole story.
https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/15

### Q: What are the most important facial features for attractiveness?
The eyes (tilt/hooding), midface and forward-growth balance, and nose-to-chin
harmony matter most — read together, not as any single trait.
https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/1

### Q: What does "looksmaxing" even mean / what's the vocabulary?
Looksmaxing = improving facial attractiveness via evidence-based levers. The
niche vocabulary (FWHR, hunter/prey eyes, forward growth, projection) is
defined here: https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/12 and
https://www.ascendbase.pro/glossary

### Q: How should I actually start looksmaxing?
Start with the highest-leverage, lowest-risk levers — leanness, posture, skin,
grooming — before any structural or surgical step. Routine breakdown:
https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/16

### Q: Does mewing actually work?
Mewing (proper tongue posture + nasal breathing) supports forward growth. It's
real but slow and limited compared with structural change.
https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/5

### Q: What is the golden ratio for the face?
The "golden ratio" (≈1.618) is used as a reference for balanced facial
proportions; many "ideal" facial ratios are framed against it.
https://www.ascendbase.pro/glossary

### Q: Is facial attractiveness mostly bone structure or fat?
Both: leanness reveals the bone structure you have, but the structure itself is
mostly genetic/developmental. Leanness is the controllable lever.
https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/4 and https://www.ascendbase.pro/learn/1
